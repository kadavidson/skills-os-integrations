Exploration 01

Ground Truth

The admin layer — build the map, then let behavior fill it in

L&D admins stop managing course catalogs and start managing a living skill graph. They define what good looks like for every role, connect MCP signals relevant to their org, and watch behavioral evidence populate the picture — in real time, without a survey in sight.

Step 01  ·  Set the target

Intent first.
Not a content catalog.

Admins describe what the team needs to achieve — in plain language. Coursera resolves that to specific skills, pulls from your org's rubrics and files, then maps the actual gap. No browsing. No guessing. You get a targeted plan, not a scroll of courses.

1 Admins write the goal in their own words — Coursera maps it to specific skills and sub-skills in your org's graph
2 Company rubrics, role expectations, and MCP behavioral signals fill in the current-state gaps automatically
3 A structured plan generates — scoped to your team's actual gaps, not a generic AI curriculum

Every skill becomes a measurable target.

Exploration 02

Skills OS

The learner layer — a plan that adapts in real time to how you actually work

The learner's plan isn't assigned — it emerges from behavioral signals across their own tools. Every recommendation is backed by a specific observation. Learning is triggered by the gap, not the calendar. Verification happens through demonstrated behavior. The credential updates as they work.

Skill detail ↗

Step 01  ·  Ground truth for the learner

Not assigned. It emerges from what you do.

Every card in Marcus's plan is grounded in something Coursera actually observed — not a course someone thought he should take. Behavioral signals from Claude, Cursor, and GitHub build a live picture of where he is right now. The plan surface shows why each item is here. No mystery.

Step 02  ·  Learning at the moment of the gap

The lesson arrives because of what you did last week.

When Coursera notices a recurring pattern — prompts solid but missing output format instructions — it surfaces the right lesson with that context already intact. Not a generic course. A targeted session, tied to evidence, delivered at the exact moment it's relevant.

Why this · what to work on
Hands-on lab · apply immediately
Real-time adaptation
If Marcus skips a section or breezes through it, the plan adjusts. Next session pulls from updated behavioral signals — not a static curriculum.
Gap-triggered, not calendar-triggered
The recommendation appeared because Marcus hit a specific pattern 4 times in 2 weeks. No manual enrollment. No manager nudge required.
Context already loaded
The MCP observation card in the lesson tells Marcus exactly why he's here. He doesn't have to wonder whether the course is relevant — the evidence is right there.

Step 03  ·  Verified through doing

A credential that earns itself.

Skills aren't certified by a test — they're verified through demonstrated behavior in real tools. Marcus's wallet updates live as evidence accumulates across sessions. Each entry is backed by MCP session data, not a multiple-choice score. The link is live — if a skill lapses, the credential reflects it.
